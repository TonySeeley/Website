New Cars

Saturday 28th October 2006

Its funny, you move to a new country, buy a nice new house some cars, fill the house with nice stuff Lisa is happy. :-)

Life is good, you finally get to the point when you have purchased everything, (yeah I know sounds impossible, but if you try hard and marry Lisa its possible) or so I thought!

Lisa pointed out to me last week that both our cars have now ended their warrantee.

OH NO I thought not again!

But Lisa was really good, she wasn't pushing for a new car, just stating a fact, and was saying we should start planning to replace them.

My car actually had done very little mileage, I mean I work 8 miles away and don't use it very much in summer (as I use the Harley), Lisa's though had pilled on the miles and was bashed up and dinged all over the place.

Also things were starting to break, she had an electric door on her van and it stopped working.

So we discussed what we would get instead.

We talked about it and agreed that when we moved here we got the whole car thing wrong.

I really wanted a new Volvo XC90 and Lisa wanted her VW Jetta, but after 6 months, she swapped it for the Dodge (that she kept for over 3 years).

The end result was we had two big cars.

Lisa's became the work horse but it didn't have all wheel drive so we had to keep mine for winter trips up the mountain.

So now the time to consider a change had arrived it made sense to change them both and get the right cars.

We decided to do the following:

Get Lisa a new Van (she really likes them - funny eh), but this time get a really nice one with all wheel drive and get one fully loaded.

Then for me get something smaller, cheaper and fun.

Good idea eh?

And as Lisa's new car was going to be more expensive it made sense to trade my car (that was worth a lot more than hers).

So that's what we did.

Last weekend we popped out to look, and 3 hours later drove home in a new Toyota Sienna. She absolutely loves it. Its fully loaded, leather, electric everything, all wheel drive, DVD Player for the kids, it's got the lot.

So we ended up as a two Van family and I had to drive the Dodge Caravan.

You have NO IDEA how much flak I got over this! What was I thinking trading my car for Lisa, am I mad, what a fool I look like driving a soccer mums car, etc etc.

So my idea of waiting till the new year didn't last, and one week later (today) I went out and traded her car for my new one.

We discussed what to get, and in truth I really didn't know what I wanted. We liked the idea of getting a convertible, but didn't want to spend a lot.

In the end we narrowed it down to a Beetle or Mustang convertible.

So I went to test drive a Mustang. It was really nice, the trouble was I couldn't get one with ABS (I know mad!!!), so for a laugh I tried the Mustang GT V8 4.6 liter engine, 300 bhp 0-60 in 4.9 seconds.

HUGE MISTAKE.

So today I traded Lisa's car and drove home on my new Mustang GT (sorry Nikki).

It is beautiful, sounds amazing, and its great fun driving a rear wheel drive manual transmission car again (my last was in 1981 :-).

Check out the pics anyway.

I am now of course skint, but very very happy.
