Racquetball Is Dangerous

Tuesday 18th December 2007

Those that know me know I'm not that sporty. I try of course but suck in reality at most team sports and don't enjoy contact sports at all (I mean you could get hurt).

As a result my army rugby days are over, and the odd game of football (soccer) are few and far between. I tried basketball a few years ago, but ended up with a really bad blisters that lead to celulitis and I nearly lost my leg! (yes from a basketball blister).

However, I do like racquet sports and over the years have played badminton, squash and more recently racquetball.

It appears I have an eye for the ball and can play, and since losing a lot of weight my game has improved.

You would think of course that racquetball is pretty safe. I mean, how much damage can you do on a racquetball court?

Well it appears quite a lot!

Apparently (according to Lisa) I'm quite competitive. I personally don't see this as I really don't mind if I win or lose, but if I do lose it won't be because I gave up. I do fight for the win.

Depending on work I do play quite often, I'm a member of the sports club ladder and have friends I play friendly games with too.

One friend is Dan Schuster. Dan and I have been playing each other for about 3 years. We are very close in ability (although Dan has been beating me a lot recently), and really enjoy our games.

While it's true I don't mind losing, I really like beating Dan ? (sorry Dan) and this has a consequence.

I try too hard!

How can you try too hard I hear you ask? Well to start with I regularly hit myself with my racquet when I play Dan. I know what an idiot.

I tend to over reach and do get a lot of hard shots back, but do tend to hit a leg, foot or side or even face (haha) with my racquet every now and then.

For instance last month we played each other and I hit my leg so hard, I had a semi-circular welt on my leg and a bruise for a month.

I often also like to miss the ball and accidentally punch the wall in a particularly over ambitious swing. I know what you're thinking - I'm an idiot!

Anyway, last Thursday while playing Dan I exceeded my own record for self destruction on the court.

Dan was playing really well and I was particularly sucky (which was making me mad) and in an attempt to return one of Dan's great returns, I jumped in the air to perform what I considered a winning shot.

Not only did I miss the ball, but I landed on the side of my foot and heard a loud cracking sound.

I crumpled to the floor, said something like "ouch" (ok it may have been a little worse) and rolled about on the floor for 2 minutes in real pain.

Dan asked politely if the game was over, I replied politely, yes and asked him to get some ice.

By the time he got back with ice I had crawled out of the court and was nursing an ankle with a small water melon stuck on the side.

The sports club offered to get me an ambulance as I knew I needed to see a doctor, but I asked them to call Lisa.

Lisa arrived twenty minutes later to find me waiting in a wheel chair ready for the off to the emergency room.

The accident happened at 1:10 in the afternoon, and we finally got home from the hospital at 6:30 in the evening.

After x-rays I was diagnosed with an avulsion fracture. This is basically when a tendon or ligament rips a piece of your bone off. Nice eh?

However as I had severe swelling, the ER wasn't happy assuming that was all I had and said I needed to keep my foot elevated and not put any weight on it until I had seen an orthopedic surgeon.

So I spent Friday, Saturday, Sunday and Monday on my arse with my foot in the air in a splint.

Tuesday morning I got to see the surgeon and he confirmed I was ok to put weight on it and wouldn't need a hard cast. Lisa was disappointed as she had already picked out the pink cast colour for me (very nice of her I thought).

I was given a huge support boot to wear (that looks like a ski boot) that I could have to keep on for more than 6 weeks. (Unfortunately it only comes in black - sorry Lisa)

So here I am, Tuesday evening, sitting in my chair in my big boot, feeling sorry for myself, but thankful I don't have a foot that looks like a huge pink penis.

All I can say is racquetball is dangerous.
