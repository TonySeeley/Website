Our Real Christmas Tree

Sunday 2nd December 2007

So Lisa's birthday has come and gone, which means only one thing. It's time to decorate the house in preparation for Christmas.

We have had a really nice fake Christmas tree now for 10 years, but last year we decided to get a second tree for the house. We also thought last year that as we lived in the Pacific Northwest, we should have a real tree. (The logic being here we live in what is probably one of the largest wooded areas in the United States).

Rather than buy a cut tree, we also decided to cut down our own. So last year we found a tree farm in the mountains where you could find your perfect tree and chop it down yourself.

Unfortunately though, when we went last year it was mid December and all the really cool trees were taken, so we purchased a pre cut tree anyway.

This year though we were not going to make the same mistake, and on December 2nd we set off to North Bend to cut our tree.

It had been snowing for a couple of days and the roads were not great, but we didn't want to wait a week and miss all the big ones so we pressed on.

An hour later we arrived at the farm (after getting lost on the way) and picked up a saw and went hunting.

The farm we went to was called Christmas Tree Creek Farm and had all sorts of trees including Noble, Grand and Douglas Fir's.

My favorite is the Noble Fir, as to me it just has the traditional Christmas Tree shape.

We found a whole field full of them and ran from tree to tree to find the size and shape we wanted.

This didn't take long as all the trees looked great and we quickly found a 9 foot tree that looked fantastic. Nicely symmetrical and no gaps or missing branches.

We all did some posing for pictures and I grabbed my saw to cut it down. (There really is nothing quite as manly as killing your own tree for Christmas).

However, by now it had started to rain. So I was lying on my side, saw in hand (being manly) cutting into the tree getting soaked. I didn't care though as this was definitely a manly thing to do so I persisted and got my tree.

The fact that I was completely soaked was irrelevant.

While being manly the kids had a snowball fight and offered lots of advice on where I was going wrong.

Lisa and I then carried the tree to the bailing station where the guys who run the farm wrapped the tree in a big fishnet stocking and relieved me of $70.

We all then got some hot apple cider and headed home.

Once home we got the tree in, got it in the stand and decorated it. While sitting back to admire our handy work I noted the bloody thing wasn't straight.

Although I'm really pleased with the end result I will definitely remember next year to re-cut the bottom of the tree when I get home so its level and stands straight up rather than leaning slightly to one side.

Oh well - lesson learnt - But we do have a lovely tree :-)
