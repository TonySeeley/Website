Skagit Valley Tulip Festival

Sunday 11th April 2010

Skagit Valley is north of where we live about half way between us and Vancouver BC. It's predominantly a farming community and probably wouldn't draw much attention if it wasn't for the crop it's known for.

What they grow in Skagit Valley is tulips! Millions of tulips actually. There are fields and fields of tulips of every color and on a nice day it's truly amazing to see.

Every year in spring the farms in the area have a tulip festival and open their doors to the general public (for a nice fee of course) and allow you to walk amongst the tulips and take pictures.

We went there a couple of years ago but it was a bit of a rainy day! This time we were luckier. We had a lovely warm sunny day with blue skies and white fluffy clouds and the tulips looked amazing.

Lisa, James, Abi and I headed out for the day to check it out. James was his normal charming self, totally happy to be away from his Xbox, computer and girlfriend. Actually that's a lie he complained the whole time. But Lisa, Abi and I had a great time :-)

There were loads of people there and we spend most of our time trying to take pictures without getting people in the shot. I got some lovely pictures on Abi who always likes getting in front of the camera. Lisa not so much, apparently it was a bad hair day and she wouldn't take her hat off. And James... Well he would only let me take his picture once, and he didn't even smile!

It's funny really, it took us about 90 minutes to get there, we were in the tulip fields for less than an hour and then we drove home.

But looking at the pictures I think it was worth it. James doesn't agree.
