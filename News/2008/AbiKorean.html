Abi in a Korean Dress

Saturday 12th April 2008

Some of you know that I now work in the Microsoft Mobile Communications Business, specifically on Windows Mobile.

Anyway, I won't bore everyone with the details, but we have customers all round the world. Phone manufacturers are everywhere, but a number of popular phones are made in the Far East.

In April I was given the opportunity to visit Taiwan and South Korea to present to HTC and Samsung.

As I had never been to that part of the world before it was a really great experience.

I had the opportunity to eat in some amazing restaurants and see some wonderful sights. One great experience was going up Taipei 101, which is an amazing one and a half thousand foot high skyscraper in Taipei. It's kind of bamboo shaped (hard to explain) but very cool.

Anyway, I decided to look for some nice dresses for Abi. This was much harder than you would think as most of the shops sold modern Western clothes. They even have a Marks and Spencers! (Reference to an English shop for US readers).

I looked everywhere in Taipei without any luck. So when we moved on to Seoul in South Korea I tried again.

This was much better as we were taken to a flea market in the city center. Here they had everything (almost too much choice). However I eventually found a traditional Korean dress (as modeled by Abi in the pictures above).

They had loads of colors but obviously I looked for pink and purple.

She loved the dress, promised to wear it all the time, and of course has never worn it since. Oh well it only cost $100 - sob sob...
