Museum Of Flight

Sunday 13th April 2003

Today we decided to have a day out.

It was raining all morning so we thought we would go somewhere that was undercover.

We chose the Boeing Museum of Flight.

The Museum is situated just south of downtown Seattle in Boeing Field. (Boeing field being the Boeing aircraft factory and test flight airport)

Usually the costs are about $10 per person, but we got in cheap, thanks to the Microsoft discount card. (It cost $9 for all three of us).

The Museum has a number of hangers, and a guided tour you can go on to find out all about the history of Boeing and the aircraft industry.

They had very old planes built based on the original Wright brothers designs, through to modern jets, they even had a very impressive spitfire from World War II.

Some of the planes you can climb inside, James went into a couple of cockpits.

We walked round the presentations taking loads of pictures.

They even have an old "Air Force One" the US Presidents plane. We queued up for ages to go inside and even though it was dated it was very interesting. Nothing like a normal jumbo, the actual plane was in service for John F Kennedy, through to Nixon. Jacky O even designed the loo (which was massive).

The Museum also had a lot of stuff on space exploration, including an Apollo V Command Module (the bit the astronauts sat in). There were space suits and even part of an early space lab.

They also had films showing the first astronauts in space.

In all it was a great afternoon out, a lot of fun.
