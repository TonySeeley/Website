Jingle Bell Run

Sunday 14th December 2003

For those that don't know I have been on a diet since August.

I will be posting on the site more information about the diet in the future, but I have definitely been busy. In 20 weeks I have lost 75 lbs and exercised on average 5 days a week.

When I started exercising all I could do was walk on a tread mill - pretty pathetic really.

After about 10 weeks I started jogging, nothing major and definitely not far. You have to remember before I started the diet I couldn't run for a bus, let alone run a mile.

I have also spent the last 20 weeks lifting weights, so my fitness has definitely improved.

About 5 weeks ago I started joking with my trainer that I should enter a run. I was really joking, but my trainer took me seriously.

She suggested I enter the Seattle annual Jingle Bell Run. This is a 5K (3.1 mile) run for charity (The Arthritis Foundation).

At this time (5 weeks ago) the longest I had run was about 1 mile.

Pauline Noble at work was on the same diet as me, although she is nearly at her ideal weight and has finished the diet program at the gym. I spoke to Pauline and asked her if she would like to do the run with me. Unfortunately she said yes and her husband Lee would do it too.

It was stating to look like I couldn't back out.

I thought I would get some sympathetic understanding from Lisa, but she thought is was a good idea too.

Oh dear...

So my training for the run began.

We started running longer distances and running up hills.

Two weeks before the run I brought some new running shoes and entered the run at the shoe shop. Lisa, James and Abi entered the 1K walk.

At this point the longest I had run was about 1.5 miles.

We continued my training until I tried my first 5K run on the tread mill. The machine simulated hills and I completed the run in 38 minutes (I had to walk about 5 or 6 times), but I was still relatively pleased. Everyone kept telling me though that road running was much harder than tread mill running.

I decided I just wanted to complete the run in under 40 minutes, and walk as little as possible.

It was about two weeks before the run that we all realised the date of the run (Sunday 14th December) was the morning after our Christmas party - Oh Dear.

So we went to the party and left at 11pm.

We had agreed to meet Pauline and Lee the following morning in downtown Seattle at the start of the run.

We got up after 5 hours sleep and got the kids ready. We all had special Jingle Bell sweat shirts to wear. We were supposed to wear bells too, but I didn't have any. James however wore his stripy socks with bells on.

We had a quick breakfast and hit the road.

I won't deny I was very nervous about this, I didn't want to look stupid.

We met Pauline and Lee at the start of the run and waited for our wave to get under way.

About 10,000 people were there for the run, it was an amazing sight.

Lisa, James and Abi left for their 1K walk and seemed to get back in no time at all.

Luckily the weather was great, not to cold, dry and sunny. The only down side was it was very windy.

Oh yeah, the first mile and a half was up hill too.

So finally our wave was called. We decided to go to the back of our wave so everyone wouldn't be able to overtake us.

The race started and nothing happened. There were so many people there we couldn't run. I would guess we were actually about 100 meters from the actual start line and it took us 2 minutes to cross it.

Two things happened as we crossed the line, Lee started his stop watch and we could finally run.

Lee is pretty fit and was going to act as our pace maker. We didn't want to go too fast (or slow).

I don't know what it was, but the start was great. There were so many people, and everyone was smiling and laughing. There was so much to see, the running seemed easy.

The three of us basically ran the 5K. I had to stop and walk 3 times (the first time was about 1.5 miles in) and each time we walked for no more than 20 seconds.

When we reached the final straight to the finish line we sprinted in and completed the run in 35 minutes.

Not only was I really pleased with myself, but I thoroughly enjoyed it.

We overtook so many people, it felt great.

Again, considering my fitness 5 months ago, it's amazing what I've achieved.

The nice part for me too was Lisa, James and Abi were there at the finish line cheering me on as I crossed.

We enjoyed it so much we are going to run the Seattle St. Patrick's Day 5K and later in the year we are going to attempt a 10K.

I wouldn't say I'm there yet, but I am getting fitter.
