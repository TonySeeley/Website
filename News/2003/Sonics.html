Sonics

Sunday 14th December 2003

In September James joined the cub scouts. The cubs here in the states are very different from the cubs I remember. Mind you I was only a cub for about 3 weeks, just long enough for my Dad to buy all the gear before they chucked me out. Apparently, they don't like you digging up the scout hut with a pen knife.

Anyway that's enough about me.

James is currently a Tiger Cub. He wears an orange tee-shirt and hat and a belt with beads. All very strange. Anyway he really likes it so it's all fine with me.

Something else that's very different is the family involvement.

James has to do certain tasks to win beads and then badges. Some examples are; create a food pyramid (don't ask me), create a marshmallow snowman, maintain a den scrap book, go on a hike, visit the flight museum and go to a sports event.

On some of these tasks, the family (i.e. me) has to attend.

It was decided by his den leader that the best sporting event they should attend was basketball. As a game doesn't last too long.

As a result we all went to see the Seattle Super Sonics basketball team play at Seattle Key Arena.

I should point out that the game was 2 hours after the Jingle Bell Run, which was 5 hours after our office Christmas party, so Lisa and I were really looking forward to it.

We arrived at Key Arena with about 10 minutes to spare, parking was dead easy and we found our seats in no time.

Most of James' den were already there waiting for the game to start.

As none of us had ever been to a basketball game before we didn't know what to expect.

Yeah, sure, we knew there would be very tall guys running around bouncing a ball, but we didn't know the rules, how long a game lasted or what happened at half time etc.

Our seats were very high up (they were cheap ones), but we could see really well.

We had remembered to bring binoculars with us but James wouldn't let go, so he had a great view.

The game was broken up into 4 quarters, each lasting 12 minutes. However, each team can call a timeout whenever they want (although they have a maximum number to use up - we don't know what this number is).

Every time a timeout is called or a quarter ends, all these blokes dressed up in animal costumes run onto the court and entertain the crowd.

The entertainment usually consists of some sort of competition between the said animal blokes, but as the primary mascot for the Sonics is a sassquatch called Squatch he usually wins.

Other forms of entertainment include picking on the referees, something the audience seem to love.

At half time, a wrestling ring was brought on and a bout between all the animal blokes ensued. Guess who won? Yup, Squatch, much to the delight of all the kids in the audience.

Later on, I can't remember if it was third quarter or a time out, a trampette was brought out and the afore mentioned animal blokes bounced on it and got baskets. In truth, they were quite entertaining.

Oh yeah, and the basketball was good too. And we won. Which was nice.

In truth, it was fun, but I am writing this at midnight on a Sunday and want to go to bed. So if you want all the details ask me later.
