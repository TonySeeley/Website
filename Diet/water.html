Water

Diet Experience

During the program the dietitians continually stressed the importance of drinking water. In fact they asked me to drink 64 fl oz a day (that's 8 8 fl oz glasses of water a day - which is a lot). It also had to be WATER, not tea, coffee or any kind of soda or juice - just water.

I have to say this was made much easier due to the amazing tap water we have here in Washington state. I think if I still lived in the UK it would have had to have been lots of bottled water (which of course gets pricey).

Although I no longer drink my 8 glasses of water a day, I do drink miles more than I used to. I regularly drink water when working out, drink at least 1 glass or water at work during the day and often just drink water in restaurants.

General Information

So here are some important facts about water:
  • Water is second only to oxygen in importance.
  • 70% of body weight is water.
  • The nutrients we consume from food and beverages are transported from the intestine to the blood stream by water.
  • Waste products are transported by water to the kidneys and eliminated from the body.
  • The need for water increases in hot weather or while travelling on an airplane.
The tips we were given were:
  1. Get good water.
  2. Choose a container that is convenient and appealing.
  3. Add ice and a slice of lemon for flavour.
  4. Make it a habit to drink;
    • After waking up.
    • Before a meal.
    • Prior to working out, drink two glasses.
    • After working out drink a glass.
  5. Your brain will tell you you are thirsty when you have 25% deficiency.
  6. Caffeine and artificial sweeteners will continue to dehydrate you.
  7. If you become dehydrated, your body may turn on your hunger cravings.
Good water choices are:
  1. Tap water with ice and/or lemon.
  2. Bottled water.
  3. Carbonated mineral water.
