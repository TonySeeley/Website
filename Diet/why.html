Why Diet?

As far back as I can remember I've been overweight. I think this started once I reached 9 or 10. I don't know why, I have always put it down to unlucky genes.

I had a successful period of being, what I'll call a "normal weight" in my early twenties. I always attributed this to the amount of exercise I did while in the armed forces.

In 1987 I had a road traffic accident and broke my back. This excused me from any more exercise and the pounds began to climb.

In 1986 I was 12 stone 7 (175 lbs), by the time I left the Army in 1992, I had reached a weight of 19 stone (266 lbs). To be honest, this was entirely down to poor diet and little exercise, which was mainly due to laziness; my back had healed sufficiently by then for me to exercise again. I had also been married for 4 years by this time and I guess I had become contented (I hate that word) and just let myself go.

I left the army to sit behind a computer, and worked in an environment where people would actually fetch my food so I could continue to code. Although this was productive for the company, my weight continued to balloon.

In 2002 an opportunity arose to move to the States, where lived the highest number of obese people in the world. As a guy who likes his food, things didn't look good.

In January 2003 Lisa, James and I arrived in Seattle, and, like the locals discovered the benefits of eating out. I can't say how much I weighed at this time, as I had stopped getting on scales a long time ago. However I would guess I was between 23 and 24 stone (330 - 340 lbs).

By July 2003, I had reached 24 stone 4 lbs (340 lbs), my heaviest to date. I had also just had my 40th birthday and become the proud father of a baby girl (Abi). I think the final nail in the coffin was being told by my doctor that I had dangerously high blood pressure and cholesterol and needed to go onto medication.

The time had defiantly come to make some life style changes. My concerns of course being I would not be around to see my children grow; something that was confirmed by my doctor. At best I could expect type II diabetes and future heart disease. I probably wouldn't make 65.

One of the benefits of moving to America was the health cover and support provided by Microsoft. Microsoft already provided an employment benefit of being a member of one of the best health clubs and gyms I have ever seen, but for the over weight there was more.

The health club the company used had a weight loss program, called 20/20 lifestyles. This program (more info on another page) guaranteed weight loss. The problem was the program was $9,000 (about £5,600). Fortunately (if that's the word), if you had a body mass index (BMI) of over 30, Microsoft would pay the first $6,000.

My BMI was 45, this made me what was termed "Morbidly Obese", and eligible for the Microsoft support.

So, on Monday 28th July 2003, after 3 weeks of application forms, doctors examinations and blood work, I started the program.

This part of my web site plots my successes and goals.

I have learnt so much about nutrition, diet and exercise; I felt this was a good medium to share.

If nothing else, by publishing my progress, it could act as an incentive in the future to continue to my ideal weight.

Hopefully, some of my experiences will be of interest, and even helpful to those who wish to follow my lead and shed a few pounds.
